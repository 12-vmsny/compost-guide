Composting in a New York City Apartment: A Three-Step Guide

With mandatory composting coming soon to the city, here’s how you can start doing it from your available living space.

By Valeria Morales

October 12, 2023

In June of this year, the City Council approved a bill that will require New Yorkers to start separating their food waste from regular trash. The goal is to reduce the amount of organic food waste the city sends to landfills, where it produces a potent greenhouse gas called methane.

The residential mandate will roll out borough by borough, starting with Brooklyn and Queens this October, followed by the Bronx and Staten Island in March 2024, and Manhattan that October. So whether you’re living in an apartment in the Upper West Side or in a studio in Brooklyn, mandatory composting is coming to the city whether you think you have enough living space to do it or not.
